Triple Axel

Triple axel is a jump, after a forward-facing takeoff, that involves three and a half rotations. (The axel is the only jump in which skaters leave the ice facing forward.)This is also a jump that Yuzuru Hanyu never fails.

Delayed Axel

An Axel done with not only a delay in rotation, but also a totally open air position, in which the skater doesn't pull their body in for rotation until they reach the apex of the jump's height.

Layback Biellmann Spin

The Biellmann spin is executed by the skater grabbing their free blade and pulling the heel of their boot behind and above the level of the head so that their legs are in an approximate full split, with the head and back arched upward.

Quadruple Axel

A quad axel is a figure skating jump which has four and a half revolutions. Yuzuru Hanyu challenged this jump during Beiing 2022 Winter Olympics

Doughnut camel Spin

A Doughnut spin is a varaiation of a camel spin where the skater grabs their skate from behind, the skater's head is pulled toward the free leg's foot so that the skater's head, torso, and free leg form a doughnut shape parallel to the ice. In other words, looking dwon on the shater the leg and torso form a circle or donought.

Back-counter Into 3A

A one-foot turn with entry and exit on the same edge but which results in a change of lobe with the rotation outside the original lobe and then connect with a triple axel jump.

One-handed Hydroblading

A hydroblade is a figure skating edge move in which a skater glides on a deep edge with the body stretched in a very low position, almost touching the ice, with the knee of the skating leg deeply bent, the free leg crossed behind and extended outside the circle, and the upper body leaning into the circle with two, one, or no hands skimming the ice.

Layback Ina Bauer

An Ina Bauer is a "moves in the field" element in figure skating in which a skater skates on two parallel blades. One foot is on a forward edge and the other leg is on a backwards and different parallel edge. The forward leg is bent slightly and the trailing leg is straight.

Side Lunge (Pistol Pose)

a type of lunge where the skater extends one leg to the side while keeping the other leg bent and usually behind.Starts in T-position and gradually presses forward, bending the skating knee and pressing it forward over the skating toe.

Y Spiral

A Y-spiral is performed with the skate of the free leg held out to the side. This is usually performed as a catch-foot spiral, although some skaters have increased the difficulty of this position by releasing the foot and holding the leg position unsupported.
